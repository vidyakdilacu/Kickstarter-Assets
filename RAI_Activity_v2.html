Activity 1 · Scenario

HR Agent at a fictional organisation

An HR department is deploying a generative AI agent to help employees with HR-related queries — leave balances, performance reviews, policy clarifications. The agent connects with the HR management system and other internal data sources. The team wants the agent to uphold all six Responsible AI principles. But upon review, they've identified specific challenges that need to be addressed.

Problem statement: While the agent improves efficiency, it also raises concerns. Can it be trusted to provide accurate, fair, secure, and inclusive guidance — and who is accountable when it fails?

Activity 2 · Risk to RAI Mapping

Which Responsible AI principle is impacted by each risk?

List the risks you identified earlier (in Module 1, or right now). For each risk, click the RAI principle chips that you think are most impacted. A risk can map to multiple principles — that's normal and often the most insightful answer.

This activity helps elevate your risks into a governance framework that scales. The principles give you the "why" behind every governance and design decision.

⚖️
Fairness Are we being fair to everyone? Are opportunities, resources, and information allocated equally across all who use the system?
🛡️
Reliability & Safety Does it work well even in tough or unexpected situations? Can users trust the outputs to be accurate and dependable?
🔒
Privacy & Security Is personal and organisational information protected? Can only authorised people access sensitive data?
🌍
Inclusiveness Does it work for everyone — across abilities, backgrounds, and languages — including users of assistive technology?
🔎
Transparency Can we explain how the system makes decisions or where its answers come from — to a non-technical user?
📝
Accountability Is it clear who is responsible for outcomes? When something goes wrong, who fixes it and who answers for it?
